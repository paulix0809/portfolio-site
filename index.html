Chapter 01 of 06Intro

Intro

Industrial electronic · Barcelona · 2026

A music collective built as ritual, not party.

Kømandø is a new Barcelona collective focused on industrial electronic events. A group of friends launching their first promotion with a clear vision: bring specific artists, create atmospheric experiences, avoid typical club marketing.

I designed their complete visual identity from zero — logo, aesthetic language, event flyers, social presence. Everything except promotional videos, which one member handles internally.

This case study documents the full system across six chapters: brief and context, creative direction, brand identity, campaign design, digital extensions, and live implementation.

Chapter 02 of 06Direction

Direction

Fig. 02.I · Moodboard · visual references & atmosphere

Designed as ritual, not advertisement.

The aesthetic draws from abandoned industrial spaces — underground venues where light cuts through darkness. Deep reds against charcoal blacks. Metal and concrete textures. Typography that feels forged, not designed.

Three keywords guided every decision: atmospheric, underground, ceremonial. This is not club marketing; it's an invitation into a space. The visual language speaks to those who already understand.

References: derelict warehouses, brutalist architecture, gothic metal typography. Palette: void blacks, deep crimsons, metallic highlights. No bright colors, no clean surfaces, no comfort.

Chapter 04 of 06Flyers

Flyers

A campaign built for revelation, not advertising.

Three main pieces, four designs for one event: teaser without lineup, full reveal with artists, two timetable options as the brand evolves. Only one timetable will be used, but both explore how the identity adapts.

The atmospheric photography stays constant — abandoned spaces, red lighting, industrial textures. Typography hierarchy shifts with content density. The identity flexes but never breaks character.

From mystery (teaser) to announcement (lineup) to logistics (timetables). A complete promotional ecosystem for their inaugural event.

Chapter 05 of 06Motion

Motion

Static becomes ritual. Motion becomes atmosphere.

Four animated treatments extend the campaign beyond static posts. Each animation approach matches its purpose: sinister glitch for the teaser, metallic shimmer for the lineup reveal, industrial decay for the timetables.

No smooth transitions or polished effects. Everything feels harsh, mechanical, abandoned. Flickering lights, metal textures, dark matter distortions. Motion that makes the viewer stop scrolling and enter the space.

The goal: transform promotional content into atmospheric experience. Make people feel the venue before they arrive.

Chapter 06 of 06In context

In context

The work in the wild. Event pending.

May 16th approaches. The identity lives on Instagram, TikTok, and Resident Advisor's event listings. On RA, Kømandø cuts through the noise — dark, atmospheric, uncompromising among the bright promotional clutter.

Early response confirms the approach: the aesthetic stops scrolls, builds anticipation without explanation. The work speaks to those who understand. 74 people attending and growing.

Documentation pending: event photography, crowd response, flyers in physical space. One month until the complete story.
