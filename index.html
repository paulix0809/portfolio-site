Kømandø

Year 2026
Scope Full identity
Chapters 06

A new Barcelona collective promoting industrial electronic events. Complete brand system from logo to event collateral — designed for their inaugural event and ongoing identity.

The brief was an event identity for an underground electronic night — something atmospheric enough to belong to the scene, structured enough to scale from flyers through motion to live signage. The constraint was a single accent — the metallic fragment mark — that had to hold at every size and survive formats it was never originally designed for. The tradeoff: I leaned hard into industrial restraint over decoration, trusting that one strong mark held with discipline would do more than a dozen variations.

Chapter 01 of 06Intro

Intro

Industrial electronic · Barcelona · 2026

A music collective built as ritual, not party.

Kømandø is a new Barcelona collective focused on industrial electronic events. A group of friends launching their first promotion with a clear vision: bring specific artists, create atmospheric experiences, avoid typical club marketing.

I designed their complete visual identity from zero — logo, aesthetic language, event flyers, social presence. Everything except promotional videos, which one member handles internally.

This case study documents the full system across six chapters: brief and context, creative direction, brand identity, campaign design, digital extensions, and live implementation.

Chapter 02 of 06Direction

Direction

Fig. 02.I · Moodboard · visual references & atmosphere

Designed as ritual, not advertisement.

The aesthetic draws from abandoned industrial spaces — underground venues where light cuts through darkness. Deep reds against charcoal blacks. Metal and concrete textures. Typography that feels forged, not designed.

Three keywords guided every decision: atmospheric, underground, ceremonial. This is not club marketing; it's an invitation into a space. The visual language speaks to those who already understand.

References: derelict warehouses, brutalist architecture, gothic metal typography. Palette: void blacks, deep crimsons, metallic highlights. No bright colors, no clean surfaces, no comfort.

Chapter 04 of 06Flyers

Flyers

A campaign built for revelation, not advertising.

Three main pieces, four designs for one event: teaser without lineup, full reveal with artists, two timetable options as the brand evolves. Only one timetable will be used, but both explore how the identity adapts.

The atmospheric photography stays constant — abandoned spaces, red lighting, industrial textures. Typography hierarchy shifts with content density. The identity flexes but never breaks character.

From mystery (teaser) to announcement (lineup) to logistics (timetables). A complete promotional ecosystem for their inaugural event.

Chapter 05 of 06Motion

Motion

Static becomes ritual. Motion becomes atmosphere.

Four animated treatments extend the campaign beyond static posts. Each animation approach matches its purpose: sinister glitch for the teaser, metallic shimmer for the lineup reveal, industrial decay for the timetables.

No smooth transitions or polished effects. Everything feels harsh, mechanical, abandoned. Flickering lights, metal textures, dark matter distortions. Motion that makes the viewer stop scrolling and enter the space.

The goal: transform promotional content into atmospheric experience. Make people feel the venue before they arrive.

Chapters 03

Presskits for electronic artists. One repeatable system, tuned per artist.

Three artists, three different sounds, one workable system — a presskit format reusable per artist without going template-y. The constraint was that deliverables had to be both print-quality (A4, exportable) and a live document, with clickable links to SoundCloud, Bandcamp, Instagram. The tradeoff: I fixed everything — grid, type, page architecture — except one variable, the accent colour, so each artist still feels distinct without breaking the system.

Chapter 01 of 02AEPLMV

AEPLMV

Sustainable spaces. Stronger futures.

A visual identity for the Associació d'Empresaris/es i Propietaris/es dels Polígons d'Activitat Econòmica de Manlleu i les Masies de Voltregà — a regional Catalan business association of industrial polígons, commissioned and paid. Usually this brief ends in a corporate sans and a glass-and-steel render. The constraint was that the association's pitch is sustainability, but the audience is small-town business owners — modern, not techy; civic, not bureaucratic. The tradeoff: I rendered the polígons as living things — buildings with leaves growing through them — pushing toward eco-architecture and away from generic local-government work.

FIG. 02.I Mark built from one brushed gesture — running body and heart symbol. Black shadow + red body, always in motion.
Anatomy Why this mark

Salut Games is the Olympic Games for hospital workers — healthcare staff competing in athletic events. The mark has to hold both halves at once: the athlete on the track, and the worker in the ward.

The constraint was that healthcare brands skew sterile, sports brands skew aggressive — neither tone fit. The tradeoff: a single hand-painted brushstroke for the runner, paired with a hand-drawn heart-and-cross — texture instead of polish, gesture instead of geometry. Delivered for someone I knew, the mark went on to become the brand's working identity, now live at @bcnsalutgames.

01 · Two strokes

Black shadow + red body. A runner mid-stride, motion compressed into one painted gesture. Red carries forward; black anchors behind. Speed and weight together — a pulse on the move.

02 · Brushed, not built

Hand-painted, not vectorised. This is a brand for people who heal — texture beats precision. The roughness is the point: it reads as human, not corporate.

03 · Heart with a cross

The small mark in the upper right is what pins it to healthcare. Without that heart-and-cross, this is just a sports logo. With it, the runner is unmistakably one of them.

In context Out in the world

The mark is alive — BCN Salut Games runs it across an active Instagram presence and a proposed web identity. Real audience, real reach.
